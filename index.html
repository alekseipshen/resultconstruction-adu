Frequently Asked Questions

The Shelf Ready ADU Program offers pre-approved ADU (Accessory Dwelling Unit) plans provided by the City of Sacramento and Sacramento County. These plans have already been reviewed and approved for building code compliance, which means you can skip the lengthy architectural design and plan review process. This saves you thousands of dollars in design fees and months of waiting time.

Eligibility depends on your property's location and zoning. If your property is within Sacramento city limits, you can use the City plans. If you're in unincorporated Sacramento County, the County plans apply. During our free consultation, we'll verify your jurisdiction and confirm which plans are available for your property.

Minor modifications such as interior finishes, fixtures, and some layout adjustments are typically allowed without losing the pre-approved status. Major structural changes may require additional review. Our design team can help you understand what modifications are possible while keeping the benefits of the pre-approved plan.

Construction costs vary based on the plan size, site conditions, finishes, and whether utilities need to be extended. We provide a detailed, no-obligation estimate during your free consultation. Financing options are available starting at approximately $1,500/month. Contact us for a personalized quote.

It depends on the plan. For Sacramento County plans, smaller units (Laurel and Willow at 460 sf) do not require solar. Larger County plans (Redwood, Elderberry, and Magnolia) do require solar panels. City of Sacramento plans under 750 sf also have waived impact fees. We'll clarify all requirements during consultation.

With Shelf Ready plans, the typical timeline is 4-7 months from start to finish. Permits can be obtained in 2-8 weeks (compared to 6+ months for custom plans), and construction typically takes 4-6 months depending on the plan size and complexity. Compare this to 12-18 months for a fully custom ADU project.
